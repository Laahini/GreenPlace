Types of Energy

Renewable vs. Green vs. Clean Energies

Many often get confused between the differences of renewable, green, and clean energy sources. Renewable energy, also known as sustainable energy, is any energy that comes from constantly and naturally renewed sources. These energy sources cannot be depleted over time.

Green energy is a form of renewable energy. It represents the energy sources with the least environmental impact. Most green energy sources are also renewable; however, not all renewable energy sources are considered entirely green.

Clean energy represents energy sources that do not emit any harmful greenhouse gasses or pollutants during the generation process. As a result, all green and renewable energy sources are clean; however, not all clean energy is considered renewable.

All these forms of energy (renewable, green, and clean) are great alternatives and are environmentally friendly.

Solar Panels

Converting Sunlight into Energy Powering Homes

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) cells. They're popular for residential and commercial energy production due to their sustainability and the abundant availability of sunlight.

Installation Cost

The cost varies based on location and size of the system, but a typical residential solar panel system can range from $15,000 to $25,000 before incentives and rebates.

Lifespan

The average lifespan of solar panels is about 25 to 30 years, after which their efficiency may decrease but they can still produce electricity.

Maintenance

Solar panels require minimal maintenance, mostly cleaning a few times a year and periodic checks by a professional to ensure everything is functioning correctly.

Wind Turbines

Wind Power Powering Homes

Wind turbines harness wind energy to generate electricity, offering a renewable and clean power source. Wind turns the turbine blades, which spin a generator to produce electricity.

Installation Cost

Small wind turbines for residential use can cost between $10,000 to $70,000, depending on size and installation requirements.

Lifespan

Well-maintained wind turbines can last 20 years or more, with some components like the tower lasting longer.

Maintenance

Regular maintenance includes lubrication, inspection of blades and electrical components, and monitoring for optimal performance.


Learn About Wind Energy
Water Turbines

Converting Water Movement into Energy Powering Homes

Hydroelectric power systems generate electricity by harnessing the energy of flowing water, such as rivers or streams. Water flowing through turbines spins them to generate electricity, which is then transmitted to homes.

Installation Cost

Installation costs for small-scale hydroelectric systems can range from $10,000 to $50,000 or more, depending on the site's characteristics.

Lifespan

Well-maintained hydroelectric systems can last 50 years or more, with some components needing replacement or refurbishment over time.

Maintenance

Hydroelectric systems require regular maintenance of turbines, electrical components, and water intake structures.


Learn About Hydropower
Geothermal Heat Pumps

Earth into Energy Powering Homes

Geothermal heat pumps use the stable temperature of the earth below the surface to heat and cool homes efficiently. Heat pumps transfer heat between the ground and the home, providing heating in winter and cooling in summer.

Installation Cost

Installation costs range from $10,000 to $25,000, depending on system size and complexity.

Lifespan

Geothermal heat pumps can last 20 to 25 years for the internal components, while the ground loop can last 50 years or more.

Maintenance

Geothermal heat pumps have fewer moving parts compared to traditional HVAC systems, resulting in lower maintenance needs. Regular inspections and filter changes are recommended.
