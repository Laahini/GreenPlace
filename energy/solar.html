What

What is Solar Energy

Solar power is powered by energy from the Sun. This is then converted into thermal or electrical energy. Solar energy is considered to be one of the cleanest and most abundant renewable energy sources available in the world. They can be used for a variety of purposes, ranging from generating electricity, providing light or a comfortable interior environment, and heating water.

How

How do Solar Panels Work?

There are two common methods to turn the sun's power into electricity.

The first way is using photovoltaics (PV). When rays from the Sun shine onto a solar panel, the Sun’s energy is absorbed by the photovoltaic cells in the panel. This creates electrical charges that move corresponding to an internal electrical field inside the cell, causing electricity to flow.

The second way is concentrating solar power (CSP), mostly used in big power plants. It uses mirrors to focus sunlight onto special receivers, heating up a fluid, which then makes steam. This steam is used to spin a generator thus converting solar energy into electricity.

Step 1

Solar Panel Absorption

Solar panels consist of photovoltaic cells that absorb sunlight and convert it into direct current (DC) electricity through the photovoltaic effect.

Step 2

Inverter Conversion

The DC electricity produced by the solar panels is then sent to an inverter, which converts it into alternating current (AC) electricity, suitable for use in homes.

Step 3

Electrical Panel Integration

The converted AC electricity flows from the inverter to the electrical panel of the home, where it can be distributed to power various appliances, lighting, and other devices.

Step 4

Home Utilization

The electricity generated by the solar panels is used to meet the immediate energy needs of the home occupants, effectively reducing reliance on grid-supplied electricity.

Why Solar?

Solar Power - The Top Green Energy

Solar power can significantly reduce or even eliminate electricity bills, providing long-term cost savings as sunlight is free and abundant.

Solar energy is clean and renewable, producing no greenhouse gas emissions or air pollutants, thus helping to combat climate change and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.

By generating your own electricity, you become less dependent on traditional energy sources and less susceptible to fluctuations in energy prices.

Homes with solar panels typically sell for more and faster than those without, as buyers value the energy savings and environmental benefits associated with solar power.

Solar power diversifies the energy mix, enhancing energy security by reducing reliance on imported fossil fuels and centralized power plants.

Incentives

Tax Credits and Rebates

Tax credits, available at federal and often state levels, allow individuals and businesses to offset a portion of their solar investment costs against their tax liabilities. These credits can substantially reduce the upfront expenses associated with installing solar panels, making renewable energy more accessible. Additionally, rebates offer direct cash incentives or discounts upon purchase, further mitigating the initial financial outlay.

The federal residential solar energy credit is a tax credit that can be claimed on federal income taxes for a percentage of the cost of a solar PV system paid for by the taxpayer. (Other types of renewable energy are also eligible for similar credits but are beyond the scope of this guidance.)

Savings and Cost

Funding Solar Panels

Initial costs for solar energy usage for homeowners within the United states is around $2.50 per Kilowatt, or about $11,500 for a six kilowatt system. Important things to note however is that the average cost of solar has gone down every year since 2009, and that these prices vary from state to state. However this initial cost is worthwhile as solar has an extremely high return on investment at around 10%, and within a few years a six kilowatt solar power system saves the typical household about $1,500 per year. Electricity companies will also offer bonuses for excess electricity your solar panels generate.

The amount of money you can save with solar depends upon how much electricity you consume, the size of your solar energy system, and how much power it is able to generate given the direction your roof faces and how much sunlight hits it. Even though there are multiple factors that contribute to determining the savings solar panels make, on average about $100 - $150 is saved every month. Solar panels are known as great investments because they last upto 25 to 30 years and it only takes 7 to 10 years to pay for themselves. This allows for 15 to 23 years of passive savings.
