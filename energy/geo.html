What

What is Geothermal Energy

Geothermal energy utilizes heat from within the Earth to generate energy It is essentially thermal energy extracted from the Earth’s crust and combines energy from the formation of Earth and from radioactive decay.

Heat from Earth’s interior generates surface phenomena such as lava flows, geysers, fumaroles, hot springs, and mud pots, which then are used for geothermal energy. Geothermal energy is used to heat baths, heat buildings, and for generating electricity. By using steam from geothermal sources, we can generate clean electricity.

How

How does Geothermal Energy Work?

Geothermal energy plants utilize the Earth's natural heat to generate electricity in a sustainable and environmentally friendly manner. These plants typically tap into underground reservoirs of hot water or steam, accessed through wells drilled deep into the Earth's crust. Once the hot water or steam is brought to the surface, it is channeled through a series of pipes to a turbine. The high-pressure steam or water from the reservoir is directed onto the turbine blades, causing them to spin. The turbine is connected to a generator, which converts the rotational energy into electricity. After passing through the turbine, the steam or water is condensed back into liquid form and either re-injected into the reservoir to maintain pressure or released into the environment.

Geothermal power plants can operate continuously, providing a reliable source of renewable energy with minimal greenhouse gas emissions and environmental impact.

Step 2

Heat Extraction

The process begins with the extraction of heat from underground reservoirs through wells drilled deep into the Earth's crust. Hot water or steam is brought to the surface using these wells.

Step 3

Electricity Generation

The hot water or steam from the reservoir is directed onto turbine blades, causing them to spin. The spinning turbine is connected to a generator, which converts the kinetic energy into electricity. This electricity is then transmitted through power lines to homes and businesses.

Step 4

Transmission and Distribution

The generated electricity is transmitted through high-voltage power lines to substations, where the voltage is reduced for distribution to homes. Distribution lines then carry the electricity to individual homes and buildings.

Step 2

Home Utilization

Geothermal heat pumps are commonly used for heating and cooling purposes. These systems circulate a fluid through underground pipes, absorbing heat from the Earth in winter to warm the indoors and releasing heat into the Earth in summer for cooling.
